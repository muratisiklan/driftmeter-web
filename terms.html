9. Measurement Accuracy Disclaimer

NO GUARANTEE OF ACCURACY: DriftMeter does not guarantee the accuracy, precision, or reliability of any measurements, calculations, or metrics displayed in the App. All data is provided for informational and entertainment purposes only.

9.1 Algorithmic Calculations

DriftMeter employs advanced mathematical and physics-based algorithms to estimate drift metrics, including:

  • Kalman filtering: Statistical estimation techniques to reduce sensor noise and predict vehicle state
  • Sensor fusion: Combining data from multiple sensors (GPS, accelerometer, gyroscope) to improve estimates
  • GPS-IMU time alignment: Cross-correlation algorithms to synchronize location and motion data streams
  • Yaw rate integration: Mathematical integration of gyroscope readings to estimate drift angle
  • Speed derivation: Calculations based on GPS position changes and Doppler measurements

These algorithms are based on sound mathematical principles and physics models; however, they are approximations that operate on imperfect real-world data. No algorithm can guarantee 100% accuracy.

9.2 Consumer Device Sensor Limitations

DriftMeter relies on sensors built into consumer smartphones, which have inherent limitations:

  • GPS accuracy: Consumer GPS typically has accuracy of 3-10 meters, with potential for larger errors in certain conditions
  • Sensor calibration: Device sensors may be improperly calibrated or degrade over time
  • Sampling rates: Consumer devices may not maintain consistent sensor sampling rates under all conditions
  • Hardware variations: Different device models and manufacturers have varying sensor quality and characteristics
  • Sensor drift: Gyroscopes and accelerometers can experience cumulative drift errors over time
  • Processing delays: Device processing load can introduce timing inconsistencies in sensor readings

9.3 External Factors Affecting Measurements

Numerous external factors beyond our control can significantly impact measurement accuracy:

  • GPS signal quality: Buildings, trees, terrain, tunnels, and atmospheric conditions can degrade GPS accuracy
  • Electromagnetic interference: Electronic equipment, power lines, and other devices can affect sensor readings
  • Device mounting: Phone position, orientation, and stability in the vehicle affect sensor data quality
  • Vehicle dynamics: Suspension movement, vibrations, and body roll can introduce noise into measurements
  • Temperature: Extreme temperatures can affect sensor performance and battery-related processing throttling
  • Surface conditions: Road surface, grip levels, and inclines are not accounted for in calculations
  • Multi-path GPS errors: Signal reflections from nearby surfaces can cause position jumps

9.4 Not Suitable for Professional Use

DriftMeter metrics are NOT suitable for:

  • Professional motorsport timing or official competition scoring
  • Vehicle safety assessments or engineering analysis
  • Insurance, legal, or liability determinations
  • Any application requiring certified or calibrated measurements
  • Comparison with professional telemetry systems

For professional applications, use dedicated motorsport telemetry equipment with certified accuracy specifications.
